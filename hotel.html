HOTEL SECURITY

Central Protection’s Hotel Security Guard Services combines “gatekeeper” and front desk responsibilities to ensure that the needs of both property management, and tenant and resident security, are met. That is to say, These services are available 24 hours per day, 7 days per week. Our concierge services are mix of security officer and building ambassador duties provide to your tenants and residents. In Short, We extend the functionality of your in-house building services team. To clarify, We are NOT the typical “security guards”, wearing arm patches, whose main function is to control access into buildings and facilities. Our Hotel Security Guard Services is based on our “customer partnership” philosophy and our ability to tailor a courtesy officer, or front desk services, program to fit the specific building security needs our Customers’ properties. Central Protection realizes the first person your residents, tenants, and visitors are likely to encounter upon entering the property is the concierge, or front desk attendant. Central Protection Services employees are chosen to reflect a professional image, while being the “eyes and ears” of the property. We also offer a wide selection of uniforms, all custom altered, to create a positive reflection on your property and we also offer other services like Logistics & Warehouse Security and more. Above all, Our customers have the ability to choose which uniform style best reflects the property’s personality. Each employee undergoes a thorough background check, including criminal history and drug screening. Moreover, Our Area Manager works with our customers to create a complete set of concierge building instructions, which is include the site training for all assign attendants.

Our bank security guard services, including:

Attending front-desk

Announcing / signing in visitors to property

Notifying designated Customer personnel (and authorities, as appropriate) of emergencies or unsafe situations in the building, determined by Customer procedures

Answering telephones, taking messages, or transferring calls to appropriate Customer personnel

Completing daily event log detailing events of their shift

Documenting any unusual events or situations, including emergencies, on an “Incident Report” for Customer action
